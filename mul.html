Cold noodle soup

category : entree

Mul Naengmyeon is cold noodles served with a chilled broth made from beef and/or Korean radish water kimchi. The noodles are also topped with sliced cucumbers, Korean pears, pickled radishes, boiled beef and boiled egg. These add subtle flavor of sweetness, tanginess and savoriness in between them. This chilly/refreshing dish was orginally served during the winter months but can be enjoyed anytime.

prep time

20 mins.

cook time

10 mins.

serving

2 servings

instructions

step 1

Prepare the veggies. Cut the carrot into matchsticks. Thinly slice the onion. Remove the stems from the mushrooms, and slice into 1/4-inch thin strips. Cut the meat into thin 2-inch long strips.

step 2

Combine all the sauce ingredients in a small bowl and mix well until the sugar is dissolved.

step 3

Mix 4 cups of water with the packets of concentrated broth. Place in freezed for 4-5 hours or until the broth has a sluch like texture.

step 4

Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the noodles and stir. Cover and let cook for 3 to 5 minutes. Drain the noodles once ready and rince in cool water.

step 5

Combine noodles, broth, and add cucumber, pear slices, sesame seeds, and boiled egg as toppings. Serve ice cold.

ingredients

10 ounces dried naengmyeon noodles

2 packets of liquid or powdered concentrated broth

½ English cucumber, cut into thin strips

1 Korean pear

½ teaspoon kosher salt

1½ teaspoon sugar

1 tablespoon white or apple cider vinegar

1 hard-boiled egg, cut in halves

2 tablespoons toasted sesame seeds

ice cubes
